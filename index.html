About

About Me

I am an undergraduate student in Statistics with a minor in Finance at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in Hefei, China. I expect to graduate in June 2027.

My academic interests lie at the intersection of robust optimization, stochastic modeling, decision-making under uncertainty, and quantitative finance. I am particularly interested in developing statistical and computational methods for decisions under uncertainty.

My current experience combines statistical modeling, Bayesian inference, Monte Carlo simulation, and mathematical optimization, with applications to panel data, spatial coverage systems, robust planning, and quantitative investment analysis.

Research

Research Interests

I am interested in statistical and optimization methods for complex decision problems involving uncertainty, limited data, and operational constraints.

Statistical Inference

Small-sample inference, Bayesian methods, uncertainty quantification, regularized estimation, and simulation-based evaluation, with an emphasis on reliable conclusions from limited or complex data.

Stochastic Modeling

Probabilistic modeling and simulation for systems involving random behavior, rare events, dynamic uncertainty, heterogeneous outcomes, and uncertainty propagation.

Optimization under Uncertainty

Robust, distributionally robust, and stochastic optimization methods for routing, resource allocation, risk-aware planning, and other constrained decision problems.

Data-Driven Decision-Making

Integration of statistical learning, predictive modeling, and optimization to support decisions in industrial systems, urban systems, and quantitative finance.
